③ Functions of Money — 3 Core Functions
Function 1

🔄 Medium of Exchange

Eliminates the need for double coincidence of wants. Money acts as an intermediary in all buy/sell transactions.

Goods
→
💰 Money
→
Goods
Solves Barter Problem
Function 2

📏 Unit of Account

All prices expressed in money terms. Allows comparison of value across completely different goods and services.

1 kg Rice = ₹50
1 Phone = ₹15,000
Common denominator
Common Measure of Value
Function 3

🏦 Store of Value

Wealth can be saved in money form for future use. Unlike perishable goods, money retains purchasing power over time.

⚠️ Inflation Warning
High inflation erodes store of value function
Deferred Purchasing Power
④ Money Supply & GDP — The Crucial Relationship
Core Principle

💹 Money Supply Chases Output

Money Supply ∝ Expected Nominal GDP (Real GDP + Target Inflation)
SITUATIONRESULT
Money ↑, Output constantPrices ↑ → Inflation
Money ↓, Output constantPrices ↓ → Deflation
Output ↑, Money constantPrices ↓ → Purchasing Power ↑
Output ↓, Money constantPrices ↑ → Inflation
📌 Prelims Key
Money supply grows in line with Real GDP growth + Target inflation rate.

⑥ Devaluation & Inflation-Exchange Rate Link
Why Devalue Under Fixed System?

📉 Purpose of Devaluation

📦 Boost Exports
Cheaper currency → Foreign buyers pay less → Exports competitive
⚖️ Correct Balance of Payments (BoP)
Trade deficit corrected when exports rise and imports fall
💸 Attract Foreign Investment
Assets appear cheaper to foreign investors
📌 India's Devaluations
1947: $1 = ₹1 → 1993: $1 = ₹31 (multiple devaluations in between for BoP correction)

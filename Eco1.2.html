③ Productivity, Capital Output Ratio & ICOR
ICOR Planning — Worked Examples

🔢 Investment Required for 8% Growth

ICOR = 5
Target Growth = 8%
Invest: 40% of GDP
5 × 8 = 40 · High investment needed
ICOR = 4
Target Growth = 8%
Invest: 32% of GDP
4 × 8 = 32 · More efficient!
Investment% of GDP = ICOR × GDP Growth%
Also: ICOR = Investment% ÷ GDP Growth% | Lower ICOR = More efficient capital use
🧠 Memory Chain
Productivity↑ → COR↓ → ICOR↓ → Less investment for same growth → Higher efficiency

④ Exchange Rates — NER, PPP, RER, NEER, REER
Step 1

💱 Nominal Exchange Rate (NER)

$1 = ₹70 (market rate)
Determined purely by demand and supply of currencies
📈
Indians demand $
Dollar appreciates vs ₹
📉
Foreigners demand ₹
Rupee appreciates vs $
Step 2

🍔 Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)

PPP = Abroad Price ÷ Domestic Price
Burger: India ₹35, US $1 → PPP rate = $1 = ₹35
When NER = PPP → RER = 1Currencies at Purchasing Power Parity (equilibrium)
Inflation changes PPPIf India inflates faster than US → PPP changes
Step 3

📊 RER, NEER & REER

RER = NER ÷ PPP
Measures trade competitiveness of domestic goods
IndexMeaning
RER > 1Domestic cheaper → Exports competitive
RER = 1PPP Equilibrium
RER < 1Domestic expensive → Exports hurt
REER ↑Real appreciation → Exports less competitive
REER ↓Real depreciation → Exports competitive

⑨ Top 5 Prelims Traps 🧠
⚠️ Don't Fall For These
1
SEBI became STATUTORY under the 1992 Act — not 1988. It was merely set up in 1988 as a non-statutory body.
2
SCORES is an online complaint platform — not a court or tribunal. It's SEBI's grievance redressal mechanism.
3
SAT is NOT internal to SEBI — it's a separate quasi-judicial body you appeal to against SEBI orders.
4
BRSR is MANDATORY for top listed companies — not voluntary. Mandated for ESG transparency.
5
SEBI regulates all MIIs — Exchanges + Clearing Corps + Depositories (not just stock exchanges).
📈
The Core Connecting Idea
India built a statutory framework for capital market governance via the SEBI Act 1992. SEBI's 3-pronged mandate — Regulate, Protect, Develop — keeps markets transparent, investors safe, and innovation alive.
RBI owns the money market. SEBI owns the capital market. Together they form India's financial regulatory backbone.

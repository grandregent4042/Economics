Concept Comparisons — Exam Traps

🎯 UPSC Distinguisher Points

SARFAESI vs IBC
SARFAESI = asset seizure by banks; IBC = insolvency resolution by NCLT with CoC vote
ARC vs Bad Bank (NARCL)
ARC = private; NARCL = government-promoted; NARCL has sovereign guarantee on SRs
Bad Bank Shifts vs Solves NPAs
NARCL only transfers NPA — economy-wide stressed assets remain same
ICA 75%+60% vs CIRP CoC 66%
RBI framework needs 75% value + 60% number; IBC CoC needs only 66% by value
Section 35AA vs 35AB
35AA = Govt authorizes RBI for specific cases; 35AB = RBI can issue general directions
NARCL vs IDRCL
NARCL acquires the NPA; IDRCL manages & resolves it — two-entity bad bank model
🏦
The Connecting Thread
When a bank loan goes bad, India has a layered response system: SARFAESI (seize collateral) → ARC (sell to specialist) → NARCL/Bad Bank (centralize & shift NPAs) → RBI Circular framework (inter-creditor coordination) → IBC (insolvency via NCLT). The 2017 Banking Regulation Act gave RBI legal authority to push cases into IBC, but the 2019 Supreme Court judgment limited this to case-specific, Govt-authorized directions only. The goal throughout: clean bank balance sheets → restore credit flow → financial stability.
⚡ SARFAESI 2002 🏢 ARC Model 🏦 NARCL 2021 📋 15% Cash + 85% SRs 🗳️ ICA 75%+60% ⚖️ SC 2019 S.35AA 🔄 Shift ≠ Solve

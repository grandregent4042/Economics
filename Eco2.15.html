① Disinvestment vs Strategic Disinvestment — Key Difference
Plain Disinvestment

📉 Govt Sells Some Shares

Government reduces stake in PSU
May still keep majority ownership
Management control stays with govt
Day-to-day operations unchanged
Done via IPO or direct sale
Stock market listing or bilateral deal
Example
Govt sells 5–10% stake in Coal India via OFS — still retains 75%+ and management control.
✅ Partial ownership sale ✅ Govt retains control
Strategic Disinvestment 🔥

🏭 Privatisation-Like Transfer

Substantial portion sold (up to 50%+)
Significant majority stake transferred
Management control also transferred
Private buyer runs the company
Strategic private buyer selected
Not just financial investor
🔥 Key Prelims Fact
Strategic Disinvestment = Share sale + Management control transfer. It is essentially a form of privatisation.
🔴 Privatisation-like 🔴 Control given away

📌 The 4 Strategic Sectors

1️⃣ Atomic Energy · Space · Defence 2️⃣ Transport · Telecom 3️⃣ Power · Petroleum · Coal · Minerals 4️⃣ Banking · Insurance · Financial Services
🔥 Key Rules
Strategic sectors → only min. PSU retained · Non-strategic → privatize or close · Other PSUs cannot bid during strategic disinvestment.
🏭
The Core Connecting Idea
Govt wants to minimize its footprint in business → Identifies PSU (via NITI Aayog) → DIPAM coordinates → CCEA approves → Strategic buyer selected → Shares + management transferred → Govt earns revenue → Efficiency improves → Competition increases → Consumer benefits. In 4 strategic sectors — only minimal PSU presence retained. In all others — privatize or close.
📌 DIPAM = Nodal ✅ CCEA = Approves 🔢 4 Strategic Sectors ⚠️ Profit ≠ Efficiency 🚫 Other PSUs Can't Bid 🔄 = Privatisation

⑥ Monetary Concepts — CRR & SLR

📊 CRR vs SLR — Key Reserve Requirements

ConceptFull FormWhat Banks DoInterest EarnedPre-1991 Impact
CRR Cash Reserve Ratio Keep portion of deposits with RBI as cash ❌ No interest paid Very high → Drained profitability
SLR Statutory Liquidity Ratio Keep portion in approved govt. securities ✅ Low interest paid Was very high → Forced into govt bonds, less loans
⚠️ Before 1991: Very high CRR + SLR meant banks had little money left to lend freely → Inefficiency → Crisis. Narasimham I recommended reducing SLR to 25%.

The Connecting Thread
India moved from colonial banking (serving British interests) → nationalized banking (serving development goals) → inefficient over-controlled banking → liberal market-oriented reforms (1991) → governance & inclusion reforms (2013–2022). The challenge now: Making PSBs efficient, well-governed, and financially inclusive simultaneously.

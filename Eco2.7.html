The Core Connecting Idea
Banks must give 40% credit to priority sectors. If they fail, the shortfall goes to RIDF managed by NABARD. RBI introduced on-lending & co-lending models to expand reach via NBFCs. The Lead Bank Scheme (1969) coordinates district credit. KCC (1998-99) gives farmers timely credit. The Govt provides 4% effective rate via Interest Subvention. RBI monitors via reports and quarterly surveys for monetary policy decisions.
📊 SCBs = 40% 🌾 RRBs = 75% 🔗 Co-lending 2020 💳 KCC 1998-99 💸 ISS = 4% effective 🏗️ RIDF → NABARD

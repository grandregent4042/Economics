① What Is GDP?
Core Definition

🌍 GDP Defined

GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT
Final Value of Goods & Services
Produced WITHIN Domestic Territory · In ONE Year
✅ FINAL goods only ❌ No intermediate goods ✅ Domestic Territory ✅ One Financial Year
Key Concepts

📌 Why "Final" Goods?

Avoids Double Counting
Intermediate goods already included in final price
Example: Wheat → Bread
Wheat (₹100) → Baker adds ₹150 → GDP = ₹250, not ₹300
Value Added Method
Each stage: output minus input = value added
🔑 Three Faces of GDP
Output = Income = Expenditure → All give SAME GDP

② Three Methods of Calculating GDP
Method 1 — Production Side

🏭 Value Added Method

Step 1: Find Output of each firm
Total value of goods produced
Step 2: Subtract Intermediate Inputs
Raw materials purchased from others
Step 3: Sum across all firms
GDP = Σ Value Added
FARMER → BAKER EXAMPLE
GDP = ₹100 + ₹150 = ₹250
Farmer VA: ₹100 | Baker VA: 200−50 = ₹150
Method 3 — Income Side

💵 Income Method

GDP = SUM OF FACTOR INCOMES
W + R + I + P

🛒 Expenditure Method

⑤ Savings–Investment Identity
Derivation — Step by Step

🔁 From GDP to S = I

Household Income Split
GDP = C + S + T (consume, save, pay tax)
Expenditure Equation
GDP = C + I + G + X − M
Equate Both
C + S + T = C + I + G + X − M
Cancel C → Rearrange
(I − S) + (G − T) = M − X
SPECIAL CASE: No Govt, No Foreign Sector
I = S
Investment equals Savings · Classic Identity
Discrepancy — Why Methods Don't Match

📊 The Discrepancy Problem

Theory vs Reality
In theory: Production = Income = Expenditure. But in practice, data collection gaps cause mismatches.
Data Gaps
Incomplete survey data in some sectors
Informal Economy
Unrecorded transactions in unorganised sector
Timing Differences
When goods produced vs when sold
⚠️ NSO shows "Discrepancy" line in GDP tables — UPSC sometimes asks this!
⑥ GDP vs GNP vs NDP vs NNP — Comparisons

📐 The Aggregates Table — National Accounts

Aggregate Full Form Formula Key Difference
GDP at MP Gross Domestic Product at Market Price C + I + G + (X−M) Domestic + Market prices (includes taxes)
GNP at MP Gross National Product at Market Price GDP + NFIA Adds net factor income from abroad
NDP at MP Net Domestic Product at Market Price GDP − Depreciation Removes capital consumption (wear & tear)
NNP at MP Net National Product at Market Price GNP − Depreciation National + Net of depreciation
NNP at FC National Income NNP at MP − Net Indirect Taxes = National Income (UPSC love this!)
💡 NFIA = Net Factor Income from Abroad = Factor income earned by Indians abroad − Factor income by foreigners in India

The Core Connecting Idea
GDP has three equivalent faces. Production = Income = Expenditure — all measure the same economic activity from different angles. NSO uses Value Added + Expenditure methods. The key challenge: discrepancy arises due to data gaps. India's GDP = Σ SGDP + Centre output + Domestic Territory activities.

⑩ The Big Picture — Core Connecting Idea
🏛️

Crisis → Compulsion → Change

India's economic reforms were not planned — they were forced. The 1991 BoP crisis compelled India to abandon 40 years of state-controlled, inward-looking policy. The LPG reforms unleashed growth, reduced poverty, but left agriculture behind and deepened inequality. The journey from 3.5% Hindu Rate of Growth → 6-8% modern growth was painful but transformative.

Step 2 — Key Ratios

📐 CDR & RDR

CDR — Currency Deposit Ratio
= Currency held by public ÷ Deposits
Behaviour of the public — how much they keep as cash vs. bank
RDR — Reserve Deposit Ratio
= Reserves ÷ Deposits = CRR + SLR
Set by RBI policy — how much banks keep vs. lend
Example: cdr = 1 (half cash, half bank) · rdr = 0.2 (keep 20%, lend 80%)
Step 3 — Fractional Reserve

🔄 The Creation Loop

4

Money supply expands beyond base!
⑤ Money Multiplier Formula
The Formula

🧮 Money Multiplier

MONEY MULTIPLIER (MM) =
1 + cdr
cdr + rdr
With cdr=1, rdr=0.2:
(1+1) / (1+0.2) = 2/1.2 = 5/3 ≈ 1.67
Money Supply = (5/3) × Monetary Base
Multiplier Behaviour

📉 What Changes the Multiplier?

↑ CDR (public holds more cash)
→ Money Multiplier ↓ (less deposited)
↑ RDR (banks keep more reserve)
→ Money Multiplier ↓ (less lent)
↑ CRR (higher RBI mandate)
→ RDR increases → Multiplier ↓
↑ SLR (more govt securities)
→ RDR increases → Multiplier ↓
📌 RDR = CRR + SLR (both set by RBI policy)

The One-Line Story of Money
RBI prints money (M0) → Public deposits in banks → Banks keep fraction (CRR+SLR) & lend rest → Loans create more deposits → Money supply becomes a multiple of base money.
Higher CRR/SLR = smaller multiplier = less money created. Lower CDR (more banking) = larger multiplier = more money created.
M0 → Source M3 → Measure MM = (1+cdr)/(cdr+rdr) CRR & SLR → Controls Bond Buy → MS ↑ Tax → MS ↓

③ Deficits — The Real Game
Deficit 1 of 4

📉 Revenue Deficit

Revenue Deficit = Revenue Expenditure − Revenue Receipts
WHEN GOVT SPENDS MORE ON CONSUMPTION THAN IT EARNS
⚠️ Why Dangerous?
Forces govt to cut capital spending for operations
📉 Impact 1
Less money for roads, infra, development
📉 Impact 2
Borrowing for current consumption = debt trap
❌ No asset created from deficit ❌ Reduces growth potential
Deficit 2 of 4 — MOST IMPORTANT

💥 Fiscal Deficit

Fiscal Deficit = Total Expenditure − Total Receipts (excl. borrowings)
= TOTAL BORROWING BY GOVERNMENT THIS YEAR

Fiscal Deficit
ALL BORROWING
⚠️ #1 Prelims Trap
Borrowing is included in capital receipts, but excluded when computing fiscal deficit. FD literally equals how much the govt must borrow.
Deficit 3 of 4

🔮 Primary Deficit

Primary Deficit = Fiscal Deficit − Interest Payments
CURRENT YEAR STRESS WITHOUT PAST DEBT BURDEN
📊 What it shows
If FD exists even after removing inherited debt cost
🎯 Purpose
Measure PRESENT fiscal stress, not historical
✅ Primary Deficit = 0
Means govt borrows ONLY to pay past interest
Interest payments = Revenue Expenditure (not Capital!)
Deficit 4 of 4 — Special Concept

🌟 Effective Revenue Deficit

ERD = Revenue Deficit − Grants for Capital Asset Creation
TRUER PICTURE OF CONSUMPTION DEFICIT
🔑 Why It Exists
Some grants to states are technically revenue expenditure but create physical assets (MGNREGA roads, PMAY homes). These are deducted from revenue deficit to show the "true" consumption deficit.
Example: Grant for PMAY construction
Classified as revenue exp → but creates housing asset
ERD removes this to show real waste
More accurate fiscal health indicator
⚠️ Effective Capital Exp = CapEx + Such Grants

Prelims Quick Hits

🎯 High-Probability Points

✅ Borrowing = Capital Receipt ✅ FD = Total Borrowing ✅ Interest = Revenue Expenditure ✅ Loans to states = Capital Expenditure ✅ Disinvestment = Capital Receipt ❌ Revenue exp ≠ Asset creation ❌ FD includes borrowing in receipts ⚠️ ERD: Some grants create assets ⚠️ Primary deficit = FD − Interest ⚠️ Tax = Non-redeemable
🧠 Memory Hook for Deficits
RFPE — Revenue → Fiscal → Primary → Effective
Each one peels off a layer: Revenue is the gross; Fiscal is the borrowing total; Primary removes interest; Effective removes asset-creating grants.
ARTICLE 112 LINK
Article 112 mandates the Annual Financial Statement (Union Budget), which must show revenue and capital accounts separately. This is the constitutional basis for the entire classification.
📊
The Connecting Thread
Article 112 mandates separating Revenue and Capital accounts. Revenue items (no asset/liability change) vs Capital items (assets/liabilities affected). When Revenue Expenditure exceeds Revenue Receipts → Revenue Deficit. Total borrowing needed = Fiscal Deficit. Remove interest from FD → Primary Deficit (today's stress). Remove asset-creating grants from Revenue Deficit → Effective Revenue Deficit. Deficit financed via borrowing (market or RBI) — printing money causes inflation.
📋 Revenue = No Asset 🏗️ Capital = Asset/Liability 💥 FD = Total Borrowing 🔮 PD = FD − Interest 🌟 ERD = RD − Asset Grants ⚠️ Borrowing ∈ Cap. Receipt

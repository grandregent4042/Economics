The Core Connecting Idea

All macro concepts flow from one truth: monetary policy has limits. Fisher shows real returns determine saving behavior. When rates fall to zero, the Liquidity Trap emerges and monetary policy fails — requiring fiscal intervention. The Phillips Curve explains the inflation-unemployment tradeoff in the short run, but stagflation (cost-push shocks) destroys that tradeoff in the long run. Deflation is the most dangerous outcome, feeding directly into the Liquidity Trap spiral. Japan's lost decades = textbook proof of all these concepts.

📐 Fisher Equation 📈 Demand-Pull = Raise Rates 🛢️ Cost-Push = Supply Reforms 🔒 Trap = Fiscal MUST ⚡ Stagflation Breaks Phillips 📉 Deflation → Depression Risk

Outcomes of Socialist Model — A Mixed Story
What worked, what failed
Gains of Planning Era

✅ What Was Achieved

✅ Industrial base built ✅ Steel & heavy machinery capacity ✅ Infrastructure growth ✅ Self-reliance in many sectors ✅ Atomic energy programme ✅ Green Revolution (agriculture)
🌟 Key Achievement
India went from zero industrial base to a diversified economy with steel, chemicals, aerospace, pharmaceuticals by 1980s.
Failures of Planning Era

❌ What Failed

❌ Overregulation & inefficiency ❌ Import substitution → isolation ❌ Low productivity ❌ Poverty fluctuated — no trickle-down ❌ Agriculture remained weak ❌ No strong primary education focus
📌 The "Hindu Rate of Growth" ≈ 3.5% / year
Far below potential. By late 1980s, fiscal deficit ballooned, BoP crisis inevitable.

The Core Connecting Story
British rule created a backward, de-industrialised economy. Independent India chose a middle path — the Mixed Economy — but implemented it with heavy state control, the Mahalanobis heavy-industry model, and the License Raj. This built an industrial base but at the cost of efficiency, competition, and poverty alleviation. By 1991, the model had run its course. The BoP crisis forced LPG reforms — and India's high-growth era began.

③ Corporate Houses Owning Banks — The Debate
Background

💼 Why The Debate Exists

India Needs CreditEconomic growth requires robust credit flow
PSBs Are WeakPublic sector banks burdened by NPAs, under-capitalised
Corporates Have CapitalLarge houses have funds, expertise, reach
IWG (RBI) 2020Internal Working Group recommended allowing corporate bank ownership — created controversy
💡 Argument: Corporates could fill credit gap PSBs leave behind
Risks — Why It's Dangerous

🚨 5 Major Risks

1. Conflict of Interest Promoter lends depositor money to own companies — interconnected lending
2. Evergreening of Loans New loans to repay old → hides NPAs. Seen in ICICI, Yes Bank, DHFL
3. Concentration of Power Corporate house + Bank = Too much economic + political power
4. Deposit Risk Banks hold public deposits — if corporate group fails, depositors endangered
5. Hard to Monitor Complex group structures, subsidiaries make RBI supervision difficult

🔑 Conclusion — Principle

Core Separation Principle

✅ Strong RBI supervision ✅ Tight regulatory oversight ✅ Ring-fencing of deposits ✅ Related-party restrictions

The Core Connecting Idea
RBI has operational independence but not absolute autonomy — the elected government retains ultimate authority via Section 7. Corporate houses owning banks risk interconnected lending & concentration of power. FSDC ensures cross-regulator coordination without overriding any regulator. Together, these mechanisms aim to balance growth, stability & democratic accountability.
🏦 RBI = Limited Autonomy 📜 Section 7 = Never Used 🏢 Corporate Banks = Risky 🤝 FSDC = Coordinator ⚖️ Borrower ≠ Lender

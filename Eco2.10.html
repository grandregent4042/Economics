② Deposit Rate ↔ Lending Rate — The Banking Model
Core Banking Model

🏦 How Banks Price Loans

Deposit Rate (e.g. 8%)
Interest paid to depositors — cost of funds
+ CRR Cost
Cash Reserve Ratio — idle at RBI, no return
+ SLR Cost
Statutory Liquidity Ratio — low-yield bonds
+ Operating Cost
Staff, branches, technology
+ Profit Margin
Spread for bank's earnings
∴ Lending Rate = Deposit Rate + All Costs + Profit
Lending rate is always higher than deposit rate
Key Principle

⚡ Why Lending Rate > Deposit Rate

8%
Deposit Rate
BANK PAYS PUBLIC
>11%
Lending Rate
BANK CHARGES BORROWER
💡 The Spread
The difference between lending rate and deposit rate is called the SPREAD — this covers all costs and profit. Under external benchmark, spread changes only if borrower's credit profile changes.
✅ Public deposits → Bank earns ✅ Bank lends → Economy grows

④ Evolution of Lending Rate Benchmarks
Pre-2010

📜 Prime Lending Rate (PLR)

Discretionary rate
Banks set freely — no formula
Below-PLR lending allowed
Opaque, inconsistent
Very slow transmission
No link to RBI policy rate
❌ Non-transparent
2010 — Base Rate

📐 Base Rate System

Formula-based minimum rate
No lending below base rate
Average cost of funds
Based on all past deposits
Components included
Avg deposits + CRR/SLR + ops + profit
⚠️ Problem
Used average cost of old deposits — slow to reflect repo cuts. Transmission still lagged.
🟡 Better but slow
2016 — MCLR

⚡ Marginal Cost of Funds (MCLR)

Marginal cost of new deposits
Not historical average cost
Tenor premium added
Longer tenor = higher MCLR
Reviewed monthly
More frequent updates
⚠️ Still Imperfect
Transmission improved but banks could delay MCLR cuts. Spread manipulation possible.
🟡 Faster but imperfect
MCLR Deep Dive

🔬 MCLR = Sum of These 4 Components

💰
Marginal Cost of Funds
New deposit rates — most sensitive to repo
📦
CRR & SLR Cost
Mandatory reserve cost
🏢
Operating Cost
Staff, branches, overheads
⏱️
Tenor Premium
Longer tenure = higher cost
⑤ External Benchmark Rate — 2019 (The Game Changer)
RBI Mandate — Oct 1, 2019

📌 External Benchmark Linked Rate (EBLR)

Mandatory for new floating-rate loans
Retail + MSME sector only
Approved Benchmarks (choose one):
🏛️ Repo Rate 📋 3-Month T-Bill 📋 6-Month T-Bill ✅ Any RBI Benchmark
Formula: Loan Rate = External Benchmark + Credit Spread
Example: Home Loan = Repo + 3% spread
Reset period
At least once every 3 months
Spread changes only if
Borrower's credit profile changes significantly
Banks free to decide spread amount
But once set, can't arbitrarily change it
Why External Benchmark Was Needed

💡 Problem Solved

🔴 Problem: Banks feared reducing deposit rates
Competition for deposits kept rates high
🔴 Problem: Small savings schemes at high rates
PPF, NSC competed with bank deposits
🔴 Problem: Old high-rate deposits still on books
Average cost remained elevated
✅ Solution: Link directly to repo rate
No bank discretion — automatic, transparent
⚠️ IMPORTANT: NBFCs are NOT required to link to external benchmark
✅ Faster transmission ✅ Transparency ✅ Automatic adjustment

The Core Connecting Idea
Financial Inclusion → more people in banking → deposits grow → RBI's monetary policy (repo rate) transmits through deposit rates → lending rates → spending decisions → inflation & growth. The evolution from PLR to MCLR to External Benchmark reflects India's struggle to make this transmission faster and more transparent.

③ Equity vs Debt — Deep Dive
📈 Equity Security (Share)
Represents Ownership
Shareholder = part-owner of company
Return = Dividend + Capital Gain
Not fixed — depends on profit
Has Voting Rights
AGM, board decisions
On Liability Side of Balance Sheet
Shareholders' equity
Last in liquidation queue
📋 Debt Security (Bond)
Represents a Loan to Company
Bondholder = creditor, not owner
Return = Fixed Interest (Coupon)
Predetermined, regular payments
No Voting Rights
No say in company operations
Principal Returned at Maturity
Par/face value redeemed
Priority in liquidation
🔑 Bond Price & Interest Rate — The Core Inverse Rule
Interest Rate ↑ → Bond Price ↓
Interest Rate ↓ → Bond Price ↑
WHY: If market rate = 12% but your bond pays 10%, no one buys at full price. Price falls until Yield = 10/Price aligns with market.
YIELD = Coupon / Market Price  |  ∴ Yield ∝ 1/Price

⑨ Final Concept Flow — Connecting Everything
🔗
The Connecting Thread
Securities enable capital formation. Govt bonds (G-Secs) are the risk-free benchmark that anchors all bond pricing. When G-Secs enter global bond indices, foreign capital flows in, borrowing costs fall, and deeper markets enable corporate bond growth — all interconnected through the interest rate–price inverse relationship.

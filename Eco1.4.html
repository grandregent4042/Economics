Master Flow — Three Layers of Adjustment
① GDP vs GNP — Domestic Territory vs National Residents
GDP — Domestic Territory

🏭 Gross Domestic Product

✅ Includes — Foreigners working in India
e.g. American working in Delhi → in GDP
❌ Excludes — Indians working abroad
e.g. Indian engineer in USA → NOT in GDP
📌 Key Rule
GDP = Production WITHIN India's borders regardless of who produces
Territorial basis All producers on soil
GNP — National Residents

🌍 Gross National Product

GNP = GDP + NFIA
NET FACTOR INCOME FROM ABROAD
✅ Includes — Indians working abroad
e.g. Indian in USA salary → in GNP
❌ Excludes — Foreigners working in India
Their income leaves country
GNP = GNI (Gross National Income)
Both terms mean the same — used interchangeably in UPSC
NFIA — Net Factor Income from Abroad (Very Important)

🔄 What is NFIA?

NFIA = Income by Indians Abroad
− Income by Foreigners in India
ONLY FACTOR INCOME COUNTED
✅ Factor Income — INCLUDED
Wages, Rent, Interest, Profit
❌ Transfer Payments — NOT INCLUDED
Remittances, gifts, pensions
⚠️ Classic UPSC Trap — Remittances
NRI sends ₹1 lakh home to family → This is NOT factor income → NOT counted in NFIA

NRI's salary earned in USA → This IS factor income → Counted in NFIA
Wages ✅ Rent ✅ Interest ✅ Profit ✅ Remittance ❌
② Gross vs Net — The Depreciation Adjustment
Depreciation Concept

⚙️ Gross → Net via Depreciation

What is Depreciation?
Wear & tear of capital — machines, equipment, buildings over time
Why subtract it?
Gross figures count capital that is wearing out; Net = actual addition to economy
NDP = GDP − Depreciation
NNP = GNP − Depreciation
GNP = GNI NNP = NNI
National Income — Key Definition

🇮🇳 What is "National Income"?

⚠️ UPSC Specific — Must Remember
"National Income" officially refers to NNP at Factor Cost
Not GDP. Not GNP. Specifically NNP at FC.
Step 1: Start with GNP
Total national income gross
Step 2: Subtract Depreciation → NNP
Remove worn-out capital
Step 3: Adjust to Factor Cost → NI
Remove indirect taxes, add subsidies
③ Market Price vs Factor Cost — Tax & Subsidy Adjustment
Factor Cost

🏭 What Producers Actually Earn

Definition
Income received by factors of production — wages, rent, interest, profit
Example
Producer wants ₹100 for effort → Factor Cost = ₹100
FC = MP − (Indirect Taxes − Subsidies)
Market Price

🏪 What Consumers Pay

Definition
Price paid in market — includes taxes, reflects subsidies
Example
FC=₹100 + Tax=₹10 → Market Price = ₹110
MP = FC + Indirect Taxes − Subsidies
🇮🇳 India uses GDP at Market Price
Since January 2015, India measures GDP at Market Price (not Factor Cost). Base year = 2011–12

⑤ Nominal vs Real GDP — Price Adjustment
Nominal GDP

📊 Current Year Prices

Measures at current market prices
Includes BOTH price + quantity changes
Problem: Inflation inflates it
GDP rises even if production stays same — just prices rose
📌 Nominal GDP growth = Real growth + Inflation
Real GDP

📈 Constant Base Year Prices

Measures at base year (2011–12) prices
Removes price effect — only quantity matters
Used for growth rate measurement
Inflation should not be counted as growth
🇮🇳 India's Base Year = 2011–12
Adopted since January 2015. Real GDP = true economic growth.
GVA vs GDP — Very Important for UPSC

🏗️ Gross Value Added Relationship

GDP = GVA + Product Taxes − Product Subsidies
GVA = Value created at each production stage
Farmer + Mill + Restaurant → each adds value
GDP at MP = GVA + Taxes on products
GST and other product taxes added at top
🍔 GVA Chain Example
Farmer sells wheat = ₹100
ITC (flour) sells = ₹500 (adds ₹400)
Restaurant (chapati) sells = ₹1000 (adds ₹500)
Total GVA = ₹1000
+ Indirect Tax ₹100 → GDPMP = ₹1100
⑥ Per Capita GDP — Living Standards Measure
Per Capita GDP Formula

👤 Per Capita GDP

Per Capita GDP = GDP ÷ Population
If GDP grows 8%, Population grows 1%
Approx: Per Capita growth ≈ 8% − 1% = 7%
More accurately:
1.08 / 1.01 ≈ 6.9% (UPSC may test this)
⚠️ UPSC Tests the Approximation Logic
Per capita growth ≈ GDP growth − Population growth
Exact = (1+g)/(1+p) − 1
GDP Deflator

📉 Nominal vs Real Relationship

GDP Deflator = Nominal GDP / Real GDP × 100
Inflation ≈ Nominal growth − Real growth
If Nominal=16%, Real=11% → Inflation ≈ 5%
GDP Deflator = measure of economy-wide inflation
Unlike CPI which is consumer basket only
📊 India's GDP Growth Data
2014–19: 6–8% range
2020–21: −6.6% (Covid Recession)
2021–22: +9.1% (Rebound)
⑦ Potential GDP & Business Cycle
Potential GDP

📈 Maximum Sustainable Output

All resources fully utilized
Labour + Capital + Tech at full use
Also called: Trend GDP
Long-run smooth growth path
Grows slowly
Capital & tech change gradually
Labour Supply Capital Stock Technology
Output Gap

🔄 Actual vs Potential

Actual < Potential → Recessionary Gap
Unused capacity, high unemployment
Actual > Potential → Inflationary Pressure
Economy overheating, demand > supply
Actual = Potential → Equilibrium
Full employment, stable growth
Recession vs Slowdown

📉 Critical Distinction

Recession = 2 Quarters Negative Growth
GDP falls → Output, Jobs, Income falling
Slowdown = Growth Rate Falls (Stays Positive)
8%→4%→2% → Still growing, just slower
Depression = Severe Long Recession
Like Great Depression 1930s
⚠️ India Recession Years
1957-58, 1965-66, 1972-73, 1979-80
2020-21: −6.6% (Covid) ← UPSC favourite

The Master Memory Trick — Three Adjustment Layers
Every macro variable is just a combination of three adjustments applied to GDP:
① Domestic vs National — add/subtract NFIA
② Gross vs Net — subtract Depreciation
③ Market Price vs Factor Cost — adjust for Taxes & Subsidies
GDP → GNP (+NFIA) Gross → Net (−Dep.) MP → FC (−Tax+Sub) NNPFC = National Income

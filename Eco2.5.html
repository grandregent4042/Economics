① What Is Monetary Policy & Its Objectives
Definition

🏛️ Monetary Policy

Core Definition
Process by which RBI controls money supply and credit in the economy to achieve macroeconomic goals.
Lender of Last Resort RBI as Regulator Price Stability
Primary Objective

🎯 Price Stability

Inflation Target = 4% ± 2%
Band: 2% floor to 6% ceiling
Based on CPI (Combined)
Not WPI — very important distinction
5-Year Review Cycle
Govt decides in consultation with RBI
⚠️ Failure = Inflation >6% OR <2% for 3 consecutive quarters → RBI must report to Govt

⑤ Open Market Operations (OMO) & Market Stabilization Scheme (MSS)
Open Market Operations

📊 OMO — Buy/Sell G-Secs

🟢 RBI Buys Securities
Injects Liquidity
Money enters banking system
Expansionary Effect
Money supply ↑
Dovish Action
🔴 RBI Sells Securities
Absorbs Liquidity
Money leaves banking system
Contractionary Effect
Money supply ↓
Hawkish Action
Type 1: Outright OMO
Permanent — securities not returned
Type 2: LAF OMO
Temporary — repo/reverse repo based
Market Stabilization Scheme — 2004 🔥

🛡️ MSS — Sterilization Tool

The Problem MSS Solves:
Large Capital Inflow (FII, FDI)
↓
RBI prints Rupees to buy dollars
↓
Money Supply ↑ → Inflation Risk
✅ MSS Solution:
RBI issues special govt. securities → absorbs surplus liquidity → funds kept in separate MSS account with RBI (not used for spending).
📌 MSS = Sterilization = Neutralize inflationary impact of capital inflows

The Core Connecting Story
RBI controls money → MPC sets Repo Rate → Repo affects all lending rates → CRR & SLR control how much banks can lend from deposits → OMO buys/sells securities to manage daily liquidity → MSS sterilizes capital inflows to prevent inflation → All tools combine into either Expansionary (Dovish) or Contractionary (Hawkish) policy stance.

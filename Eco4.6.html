1 Basic Classification of Taxes
Type A

📈 Progressive Tax

Tax rate increases as income increases.

Example
Personal Income Tax — the more you earn, the higher % you pay.
Fair to lower income
Type B

➡️ Proportional Tax

Same fixed percentage for all income levels.

Example
Flat 20% on all corporate profits regardless of size.
Neutral impact
Type C

📉 Regressive Tax

Burden is heavier on poor — same ₹ = higher % of poor person's income.

Classic Example
Bread ₹25 (₹5 GST). Rich & poor pay same ₹5 — but poor pays 10× higher % of income.
⚠ All Indirect Taxes are Regressive
Production vs Consumption

🏭 Origin vs Destination Tax

🏭 Origin / Production

Tax collected where goods are produced. Pre-GST Central Excise followed this.

🛒 Destination / Consumption

Tax goes where goods are consumed. GST is destination-based — revenue to consuming state.

✅ GST = Destination
Specific vs Ad-Valorem

⚖️ How Tax is Calculated

📦 Specific TaxFixed amount per unit — e.g. ₹10 per litre of fuel
💹 Ad-Valorem TaxPercentage of value — e.g. 10% of product price (most GST slabs work this way)
💡 Memory Aid
Ad Valorem = "according to value" (Latin). GST is ad-valorem.

4 VAT, ITC & Pre-GST Indirect Tax System
VAT Concept

🔄 Value Added Tax — The ITC Chain

VAT = Tax only on Value Addition
Prevents cascading (tax-on-tax) through Input Tax Credit.
Step-by-Step Example (10% VAT)
A → B: A adds ₹100, pays ₹10 VAT
B → C: B adds ₹200 more, total ₹300. VAT on ₹300 = ₹30. But B already paid ₹10.
B pays only ₹20 (₹30 − ₹10 ITC)
👉 ITC = Input Tax Credit — prevents tax-on-tax
✅ GST inherited this ITC principle

5 Equalization Levy & Global Minimum Tax
Equalization Levy — "Google Tax" — 2016

🌐 Taxing Foreign Digital Giants

Why Introduced?
Foreign digital companies (Google, Amazon) earn from India without physical presence — and escape Indian tax.
When does it apply?Company has NO permanent establishment in India, but earns from digital ads / e-commerce in India
6% on Digital Advertising Revenuee.g. Google Ads revenue from Indian businesses
2% on E-commerce Revenuee.g. Amazon selling to Indian customers
⚠️ Crucial Prelims Fact
Equalization Levy is NOT Income Tax. It is charged on Revenue, not profit. Separate from Income Tax Act.
Not Income Tax On Revenue 6% Ads / 2% E-comm
GMCT — Global Minimum Corporate Tax

🌍 OECD's 15% Global Floor

The Problem
Multinational companies shift profits to tax havens (Ireland, Cayman Islands) where corporate tax is 0–5%.
Solution: Minimum 15%OECD/G20 agreed: no country can tax MNCs below 15%
Pillar 1 — ReallocationTax rights shift to market countries where users/consumers are
Pillar 2 — 15% FloorIf any country taxes below 15%, home country can top-up the tax
🔗 Connection to Equalization Levy
Once GMCT is fully implemented, India may remove Equalization Levy — as digital giants will pay 15% globally anyway.
15% Global Floor Pillar 1 + 2 Eq. Levy → May Go
